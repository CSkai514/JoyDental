Dental Implant

Missing teeth are not just an aesthetic problem; it can also greatly affect your ability
to chew and speak well. With dental implants, patients have a permanent solution
to help restore the shape and function of their teeth.

Dental Implant

What is Dental Implant ?

For some oral problems, such as missing or damaged teeth, dental implants may be used as a permanent or long-term solution. The procedure for dental implants involves the installation of a metal implant into the jaw, serving as the root of a false tooth. After the implant heals and fuses with the jaw, the dentist will attach the denture to the implant. This makes dental implants a long-lasting treatment and an effective alternative to other treatments for missing teeth, such as dentures or dental bridges.

  • 01

    As with our other treatments, each case is treated on an individualized basis.

    Initially, our dentist will always consult with you to assess your specific goals, as well as review your medical history. The dentist will then perform medical imaging, such as X-rays and other scans, to assess the health of your jaw and suitability for implant placement.

  • 02

    Once you and the dentist have chosen the appropriate treatment plan, you will undergo the fitting procedure.

    In this session, the dentist will surgically insert the dental implant post into the jaw. This post will act as the root of the false tooth, where the tooth crown will be attached.

  • 03

    After the installation process, you have to wait a few months for the jaw to heal.

    The healing process is very important in this procedure, as this is the stage where osseointegration occurs. This means that during the healing period of the jaw, the implant post will fuse with the jaw to provide a stable base structure for the denture. This healing process usually takes 3-6 months, depending on the health of your jaw.

    After the healing process is complete and osseointegration is achieved, the dentist will begin the installation of the denture on the implant. The connecting part, called an "abutment", will be attached to the implant first. Then, the dentist will attach the artificial tooth to the implant.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

You may experience some swelling, bleeding, or discomfort. This is normal and will usually subside after a day or two. Please contact us if the bleeding continues for a longer period of time.

Avoid hot and spicy food, and do not eat hard food after oral surgery. Instead, focus on soft, mouth-friendly foods.

Ensure proper oral care, but don't overdo it. On the day after the procedure, you can brush your teeth and floss, but avoid rinsing your mouth. The next day, you can start rinsing your mouth several times a day with a warm salt water mixture. This step is especially important after eating. You may need to clean around the implant as well. You can use a Q-tip and a cleaning fluid recommended by your dentist for this.

Patients who are missing one or more teeth, have sufficient jaw density for the procedure, and are in good oral health are good candidates for dental implant procedures. Patients should be aware that this process requires a long-term commitment. This treatment may also not be suitable for some people, especially those with poor jaw health such as smokers or those with certain health conditions.

With proper care, regular checkups with the dentist, and good dental hygiene practices, dental implants can last a lifetime.

Implants offer many advantages, including:

  • Improved chewing and speech abilities
  • Better facial structure and appearance
  • Durability and long-term stability
  • Increased confidence
